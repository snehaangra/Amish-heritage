The Ordnung

The Ordnung, which comes from the German word "order", guides the lives of Amish people through a set of rules. It helps maintain a traditional way of life that is separate from the outside world. These rules are not permanent and vary in different communities. The Ordnung has overcome many changes over time and is a flexible guideline for life in an Amish society. Many debates have fabricated involving the Ordnung which have led to separations and divisions into smaller groups. Members promise to obey the Ordnung at the time limits are a requirement for happiness because without it, a person will become arrogant, conceited, and self-descructive.
The Ordnung influences daily practice due to the rules that it sets. Some of the rules include forbidding the ownership of automobiles, joining the military, attending high school or college, owning television, radios or computers, or initiating divorce. Amish men and women are also expected to dress in a particular fashion. For example, Amsh men need to grow a beard without a mustache, and wear a hat and vest that is Amish styled. Women are usually seen with head coverings, and a three piece dress that consists of an apron and a cape. The clothing of Amish people symbolizes their group’s identity.
