Amish Words and Phrases

The Amish speak a version of German that is called Pennsylvania Dutch. Some Amish people also speak a variation of the Swiss and English. Amish communities use different dialects and phrases by region.

Amish Words (Alphabetical Order)

  • ach - oh
  • ach jah - oh yes
  • aendi - aunt
  • appeditlich - delicious
  • ault - old
  • awlee eppa - everyone
  • blume - flowers
  • brode - bread
  • bruder - brother
  • buwe - boy
  • dawg - day
  • dat - dad
  • deerich - silly, ridiculous, foolish
  • denki - thank you
  • dochder - daughter
  • fraa - wife
  • gaarde - garden
  • gaul - horse
  • Gotte - God
  • gute - good
  • haus - house
  • hochmut - pride
  • jah - yes
  • kault - cold
  • kind - child/kid
  • kinskind - grandchildren
  • lieb - love
  • maedel - girl
  • mann - husband
  • mater - mother
  • narrish - crazy
  • nochber - neighbor
  • oll - all
  • scholar - student
  • schwester - sister
  • sohn - son
  • verhuddelt - confused
  • wunderbaar - wonderful

Amish Phrases (Alphabetical Order)

  • ach du lieva - oh my goodness
  • guder mariya - good morning
  • gute gubbotta dawg - happy birthday
  • ich lieba dich - I love you.
  • mache gute - Have a good day.
  • rutsching around - fooling around
  • wie bischt - How are you?
  • wie ghets - How's it going?
